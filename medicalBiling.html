Medical Billing Services​

Secure, Fast, and the Most Affordable Billing Solutions

Medical billing and coding services by PracticeCare MBS are not just another billing solution to outsource medical billing and transcribe patient data into billing codes. An expert team of medical billers and coders at PracticeCare MBS ensures timely preparation of the claims and expedite the overall process to ensure that medical practices get maximum reimbursements in less time.

Feature Rich Medical Billing Service

Structured Charge Entry

A systematic charge entry process guarantees more than 98% accuracy with relevant checks at every stage to make the process smooth and accurate. Due to the maximum accuracy level we maintain, there are almost zero chances of errors at this stage.

Fast Claim Submission

PracticeCare MBS claim submission team is well experienced in electronic and paper-based claim submissions. We pay attention to the smallest details of the Medicaid, Medicare, and major insurance carriers’ claim submission formats

Reliable Claim Scrubbing

Errors in billing codes are one of the major reasons that medical claims are rejected or denied. This is why it is very necessary to find and eliminate errors before the claims are submitted to payers. Our reliable claim scrubbing ensures accuracy.

Billing and Collections

The most important segment of the billing process is the timely collection of the payment claims. If there are an increased number of outstanding claims, it can hurt the revenue targets of medical practices. PracticeCare MBS’s billing team timely follows up with each payer.

Payment Posting Process

Account receivable reports of each medical practice are very important as it provides insights into the financial health of the provider. Payment posting is the process that makes it clear that how the billing and collection process is performing and helps in identifying issue

Financial Statement Reports

With periodic payment statement and reports, PracticeCare MBS's team keeps providers updated about the whole billing process and follow up with the insurance carriers for timely payments. These reports indicate if there are outstanding bills beyond 120 days.
